Asthma Exacerbation
Emergency & Pulmonology Reference · Indonesia
ER
⚡
Near-fatal features: Silent chest, cyanosis, poor respiratory effort, bradycardia, hypotension, confusion/coma, PaCO₂ ≥45 mmHg. Immediate senior ± ICU involvement.
Pathophysiology Snapshot
🔬 Core Pathophysiology ▼
1
Bronchospasm — smooth muscle contraction from mast cell/eosinophil mediators (histamine, LTC4/D4, PGD2). Reversible with bronchodilators.
2
Airway edema — vascular leak, mucosal swelling. Responds to corticosteroids (hours).
3
Mucus hypersecretion — goblet cell + submucosal gland hyperplasia. Creates mucus plugging, basis of "mucoid impaction."
4
Dynamic hyperinflation — air trapping → ↑ FRC, intrinsic PEEP. Flattened diaphragm → mechanical disadvantage. Risk of barotrauma if intubated.
5
V/Q mismatch — shunt + dead space → hypoxemia early, then hypercapnia when severely fatigued/obstructed.
⚠️
Early severe attack: Hyperventilation → respiratory alkalosis. Normocapnia in exhausted patient = impending failure. Hypercapnia = very late/life-threatening sign.

Step-by-Step ED Protocol
🟢 MILD — Outpatient/Short Stay Track ▼
1
O₂ if SpO₂ <93% — target 93–95%
2
Salbutamol 2.5 mg neb q20min × 3 in 1st hour (or 4–8 puffs MDI + spacer)
3
Prednisolone oral 1 mg/kg (max 50 mg) — give early, don't wait for reassessment
4
Reassess at 1 hour — PEF, SpO₂, HR, RR, dyspnea score
5
If PEF ≥70% and SpO₂ >93%: discharge with written action plan, OCS 3–5 days, ICS initiated/continued
🟡 MODERATE — Observation/Admit Track ▼
1
O₂ titrate to SpO₂ 93–95%. Place IV access.
2
Salbutamol 2.5–5 mg neb q20min × 3 in 1st hour. May run continuous neb if not improving.
3
Ipratropium 0.5 mg neb q20min × 3 (first hour only — evidence only in first hour, no benefit beyond)
4
Prednisolone oral 1 mg/kg (max 50 mg) OR methylprednisolone IV 1 mg/kg if PO not tolerated
5
Reassess 1 hour: if PEF 40–69% or partial response → admit; if improving to >70% → discharge track
6
If no clear improvement at 1 hr: IV MgSO₄ 2 g over 20 min (single dose)
💡
MDI + spacer = equivalent to nebuliser if patient can coordinate. Nebs preferred in severe/anxious patients and children <5y.
🔴 SEVERE — High Dependency / ICU Prep Track ▼
1
O₂ high-flow via NRM if SpO₂ <90%, then titrate to 93–95%. Do NOT use high flow nasal as primary unless NIV set up.
2
Salbutamol 5 mg neb q15–20min or continuous (10 mg/hr neb). IV salbutamol if neb not feasible: 5 mcg/min → titrate.
3
Ipratropium 0.5 mg q20min × 3 in 1st hour combined with salbutamol
4
Methylprednisolone IV 1–2 mg/kg q6–8h
5
MgSO₄ IV 2 g over 20 min — single dose, proven benefit in severe/life-threatening
6
ECG, ABG, monitor K⁺ (salbutamol → hypokalemia), consider KCl if K⁺ <3.5
7
Alert ICU / pulmonologist. NIV (CPAP/BiPAP) may bridge to avoid intubation.
8
If deteriorating: prepare for RSI — see ICU/NIV tab for intubation specifics.
🚨
IV aminophylline: NOT first-line in current GINA/BTS guidelines. Consider only in specialist-supervised severe cases with no response to above. Narrow TI, arrhythmias, vomiting risk.
1st Line — Bronchodilators
Salbutamol (Albuterol) SABA 1st Line
🇮🇩 Ventolin® neb 2.5 mg/2.5 mL, Asmalin®, Lasal®
Nebulisation:
Mild–Mod: 2.5 mg neb q20min × 3 (1st hour)
Severe: 5 mg neb q15–20min / continuous 10 mg/hr

MDI + spacer: 4–8 puffs (100 mcg/puff) q20min × 3

IV (if neb not possible — severe):
Loading: 15 mcg/kg IV over 10 min
Maintenance: 0.1–0.2 mcg/kg/min, titrate to response
Alt: 5 mcg/min → 10 → 20 mcg/min if needed
⚠️ Monitor K⁺ (hypokalemia), HR (tachycardia), tremor. Lactic acidosis possible at high doses. IV form (Salbutamol inj) often needs dilution — check ampoule (0.5 mg/mL or 1 mg/mL depending on brand).
Ipratropium Bromide SAMA
🇮🇩 Atrovent® 0.25 mg/mL (2 mL = 0.5 mg), Ipravent®
0.5 mg neb, combined with salbutamol neb, q20min × 3 (1st hour only)
Evidence supports use ONLY in 1st hour — reduces hospitalisation in severe acute asthma. No benefit if started later. Mix with salbutamol in same neb cup.
2nd Line — Systemic Bronchodilators
Magnesium Sulfate IV Severe/Life-Threatening
🇮🇩 MgSO₄ 20% (2 g/10 mL) amp, 40% (4 g/10 mL) amp
2 g IV over 20 min (single dose)
Dilute: 2 g MgSO₄ 40% (5 mL) + NaCl 0.9% ad 100 mL → infuse over 20 min
✅ Good evidence in severe/life-threatening asthma — reduces need for intubation. Mechanism: smooth muscle relaxation via Ca²⁺ antagonism. Monitor: BP drop, flushing. Avoid if renal failure. Check serum Mg if repeated dosing.
Aminophylline IV Adjunct — Expert Use
🇮🇩 Aminofilin® 24 mg/mL amp (10 mL = 240 mg)
Loading: 5 mg/kg IV over 30 min (if not on theophylline)
Maintenance: 0.5–0.7 mg/kg/hr
Target serum level: 10–20 mcg/mL
⚠️ NOT recommended as routine first-line by GINA 2024/BTS 2023. Reserve for specialist-supervised refractory severe cases. Narrow TI: arrhythmias, seizures, vomiting. Reduce dose in elderly, liver disease, cardiac failure, CYP1A2 inhibitors (ciprofloxacin, erythromycin).
Epinephrine (Adrenaline) Anaphylaxis Overlap
🇮🇩 Adrenalin® 1 mg/mL amp
IM 0.3–0.5 mg (0.3–0.5 mL of 1:1000) lateral thigh
Repeat q5–15 min if needed (anaphylaxis/severe bronchospasm not responding)
Use if anaphylaxis component, or life-threatening bronchospasm not responding to salbutamol. Not routine for asthma alone unless anaphylaxis overlap or failed standard therapy.
Corticosteroids
Methylprednisolone IV Systemic CS
🇮🇩 Medrol® inj, Urbason® 125 mg vial, Solu-Cortef® (hydrocortisone)
IV (severe/mod-severe, cannot take PO):
1–2 mg/kg IV q6–8h (max ~125 mg/dose)

Hydrocortisone alt:
200 mg IV stat then 100 mg q6h
No difference IV vs PO if absorption intact — prefer PO to reduce complications. Onset 4–6 hrs (anti-inflammatory). Give EARLY — do not delay for reassessment.
Prednisolone Oral Mild–Moderate
🇮🇩 Prednisolone 5 mg tab, Pehacort®, Medrol® 4 mg tab (methylprednisolone)
Acute: 1 mg/kg/day PO (max 50 mg) for 3–5 days
Discharge course: Prednisolone 40 mg OD × 5–7 days (adults)
Equivalent to IV if PO tolerated. Taper NOT needed for courses <14 days. Start ICS simultaneously on discharge.
Adjuncts & Special Situations
Heliox Adjunct
70:30 or 80:20 He:O₂ mixture. Reduces turbulent flow in narrowed airways. Evidence is weak — may be considered as bridge in severe asthma while awaiting intubation setup. Not available in most Indonesian RS.
Ketamine IV Pre-intubation
🇮🇩 Ketalar® 10/50 mg/mL
Procedural/RSI induction: 1–2 mg/kg IV
Bronchodilatory + sedative. Preferred induction agent for asthma RSI.
Dissociative anaesthesia. Increases secretions — give atropine 0.01 mg/kg preemptively. Maintains airway reflexes and bronchodilation. Excellent choice for status asthmaticus before intubation.
Antibiotics Only if indicated
DO NOT give routinely. Most exacerbations triggered by viral URTIs or allergens. Use only if: fever >38.5°C + purulent sputum + CXR consolidation (bacterial pneumonia complicating asthma). No empirical antibiotics for simple asthma exacerbation.
🚨
Intubation in asthma is HIGH RISK — avoid if possible. Even post-intubation, dynamic hyperinflation, auto-PEEP, and haemodynamic collapse are major hazards. Exhaust NIV and aggressive medical therapy first.
NIV in Acute Asthma
💨 Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV) — BiPAP/CPAP ▼
⚠️
Evidence: NIV in asthma has weaker evidence than COPD. Use as a bridge to avoid intubation, NOT as definitive therapy without concurrent aggressive medical Rx.
ModeBiPAP preferred (IPAP/EPAP)
IPAP initial8–12 cmH₂O (titrate to resp effort)
EPAP3–5 cmH₂O (low — avoid worsening hyperinflation)
FiO₂Titrate to SpO₂ 93–95%
Target↓ RR, ↓ WOB, improve SpO₂, buy time
🚫
Absolute contraindications: Coma/uncooperative, vomiting, unable to protect airway, haemodynamic instability → proceed to RSI.
Can deliver nebulisation through NIV mask — use in-line neb adaptor. Continue SABA/ipratropium neb through mask.

💉 RSI Protocol for Status Asthmaticus ▼
1
Pre-oxygenate — NRM 15 L/min × 3–5 min. BVM ventilation only if apnoea (risk of ↑ air trapping with BVM).
2
Premedication: Atropine 0.01 mg/kg IV (reduce secretions if using ketamine). Lidocaine 1.5 mg/kg IV 3 min before — may attenuate bronchospasm triggered by laryngoscopy (controversial but low risk).
3
Induction agent:
Ketamine 1.5–2 mg/kg IV ← Preferred (bronchodilator + dissociative)
Alt: Propofol 1.5–2 mg/kg IV (also mild bronchodilator, but may drop BP)
4
Neuromuscular blockade:
Succinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg IV (fast offset — useful if difficult intubation anticipated)
Alt: Rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg IV (reversible with sugammadex 16 mg/kg)
⚠️ Succinylcholine: avoid if hyperkalemia risk, burns, crush >48h
5
Use largest ETT possible (≥8.0 mm adult) — reduces resistance.
6
Confirm tube with ETCO₂ + bilateral breath sounds.
🔧
Post-intubation ventilator strategy:
Low RR (8–12/min) · Low Vt (6–8 mL/kg IBW) · Longer I:E ratio (1:3 to 1:5) · Low PEEP (0–5) · Permissive hypercapnia OK (target pH >7.20) · Tolerate high PIP if plateau Pplat <30 cmH₂O. Target: allow full exhalation.
💥
Haemodynamic collapse post-intubation: Think auto-PEEP / tension hyperinflation. Disconnect ET tube from vent for 30–60 sec (decompress). Rule out pneumothorax bilaterally.

Discharge Medications
💊 Medications on Discharge ▼
1. Short Course OCS
Prednisolone 40 mg PO OD × 5–7 days (adults)
Children: 1 mg/kg/day max 40 mg × 3–5 days
No taper needed for short courses. Start immediately on discharge.
2. Inhaled Corticosteroid (ICS) — Controller
Start or step up if not already on ICS. GINA emphasises: all adults with asthma should be on ICS — SABA alone is no longer standard.
Options (🇮🇩 available):
Budesonide 200–400 mcg BID (Pulmicort®, Budecort®)
Fluticasone 100–250 mcg BID (Flixotide®)
Beclomethasone 200–400 mcg BID (Beclovent®)
3. SABA Reliever
Salbutamol MDI 100 mcg/puff — 2 puffs PRN (max 4×/day)
GINA 2024 update: Preferred reliever = ICS-formoterol (MART) for mild-moderate persistent asthma (Steps 2–4). If only SABA used as reliever, explicitly counsel to not overuse.
4. ICS-LABA (if Step 3–4 asthma)
Budesonide/Formoterol 160/4.5 mcg (Symbicort® Turbuhaler®) — MART strategy
Fluticasone/Salmeterol 250/50 mcg BID (Seretide®)
Fluticasone/Formoterol (Flutiform®)
📋
GINA 2024 key change: SABA-only therapy is no longer recommended at any step. All asthma patients should have anti-inflammatory therapy. Even mild intermittent asthma should have low-dose ICS or ICS-formoterol as reliever.
